GRADING SYSTEM

Kyokushin Jinzen Do used the Kyu -Dan system. This grading system was adopted from the Japanese Judo Master Jigoro Kano (1860-938, who invented Kyu-Dan graduation systeme in 1883. This graduation system is later used in most International Budo Organizations . Mas Oyama took the graduation system when he opened his first karate school in Tokyo 1955.

Kyokushin Budo Karate, Ju-Jutsu, Kyokushin Jinzen Do use A modulation system as an educational tool to measure our students' personal development. It can help the instructor to assess what he can expect from each student. While it in the same time can be used as a motivational factors for each student. Although graduation is not an end in itself but a tool to develop and refine his personal development.

The first black belt first Dan (Shodan) is the first Degree in Yudansha (black belts).

When you no longer have to pass the biannual graduations, have the ability to acquire bad habits. The most important aspects of Kyokushin is spirit and attitude: Kihon - including Ido Kihon (Kihon during movement), Kata - develop strength, balance, technique, speed, proper breathing in his technique, Seishin-Ryoku (willpower and proper attitude) Zanshin (awareness), Mushin (empty mind).

Kumite where you get the opportunity to test your fighting skills, under realistic forms and finally tameshiwari (penetration/break ), where you get the chance to test your trained mental strength and technique(skills). When you had passed the first black belt (Yudansha degree) graduation is something very special!
